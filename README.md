
# MARS COLONY
### Marek Bulant

#### Game summary
In the year 2100, Earth starts to be very polluted and running out of resources. People realize that Earth is unfit for living so they start to think of a solution to that problem. The solution seems to be to colonize Mars. Mars Colony is a turn-based strategy game, where the main objective is to colonize and transform Mars so people can live there. The two main issues in colonizing are, that there is not enough water and there is not enough oxygen in the atmosphere. There are also several huge corporates back on Earth, that have their own agendas on Mars. You can win their favor and thus their support by helping them fulfill their agendas. As time goes on, people on Earth start to realize the importance of your cause and humanitarian packages from corporations and from other people start to be more significant also people on Earth will research new technologies, which can affect your gameplay as well. But after a certain period, the situation on Earth starts to be so unstable that people will send less and less help, due to lack of transport ships, their own needs and after a while, they will actually start to die out and you will be left without any help. Your colony will also be affected by natural disasters such as sandstorms, floods (once you start to actually accumulate some water), volcano eruptions, and some catastrophes that mankind has never seen before. Your goal is to have a sustainable colony even when you stop receiving support from Earth.

#### Core mechanics
resource management - you have to feed your people, have resources to build crutial buildings...
politics - you need to win the favor of corporations 
managing cities (oxygen and water) - your cities need oxygen for people either from the atmosphere or you can somehow isolate the city in a greenhouse or use another technology you get along the way
people's work management - you have to assign jobs to your people so your yields are as effective as possible

#### Gameplay
turn 1
You start a game and are presented with a hex map full of empty sand places. You are also granted one cargo ship, greenhouse, excavator, personnel counting 10 people and food for 20 turns to feed 10 of your people. You place your greenhouse somewhere and assign 5 workers there (the more workers you assign, the more productive it will be). You send an excavator with the remaining people to discover what is under the sand right next to your greenhouse and end your turn.
turn 3
Your excavator finds a weird mineral and you assign one person, to bring it back to Earth for further research. Then you assign the excavator to another place.
turn 5 
Your cargoship gets home and you immediately get an offer from one of the major corporates that they will provide specialized drills and personnel if you keep bringing some amount of this mineral. You accept and they put a drill and 5 miners on a ship and send it to you.
turn 6
Excavator finds fertile soil that will speed up your agriculture in the future.

#### Music
In the early game, you listen to calm and rather simple orchestra songs so you as a player feel curious and ready to explore a new planet. The more the game progresses, the more industrial and epic the soundtrack becomes. You hear epic civilization game style orchestra. In the endgame you hear more catastrophic and dramatic music, which implies that the stakes are high, mankind's destiny lies on your shoulders and there is little room for error.

#### Art Style
The whole game you see only your game map from above. Every object in the game has its own pictogram. These pictograms are hand drawn and might have some slight animations. The game is bright from the beginning but the general tone changes according to how well you are doing. If you lose a lot of people over a period of several turns, the general tone gets darker and more depressing.
